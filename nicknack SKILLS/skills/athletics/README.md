# Athletics

Used for climbing and swimming, as well as moving heavy objects and breaking things. Uses str.

<details>

<summary>Climb</summary>

Assuming ideal climbing conditions, no check is required, such as when climbing an average tree. Spaces you climb through are considered difficult terrain. \
With athletics proficiency, you climb at full speed.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Swim</summary>

Assuming ideal climbing conditions, no check is required, such as when swimming in still water. Spaces you swim through are considered difficult terrain. \
With athletics proficiency, you swim at full speed.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Brute Force</summary>

For when there are no lockpicks or beasts of burden, you have elbow grease. The DC to break open a door is based on its material and reinforcements. You can also use athletics to represent bursts of adrenaline that allow you to surpass your normal carry weight to push over statues and punch boulders into lava.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Catch Fall (P)</summary>

As an immediate action, you can attempt to grab onto something to halt your falling, or to prevent an ally within reach from falling. The default DC to do this is 20. Creatures falling farther than 30 feet increase this DC even higher.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Aquatic Combatant (P)</summary>

With athletics proficiency, you take no penalty to fighting underwater with melee weapons.

</details>

## <mark style="color:green;">Signature Skill</mark>

<details>

<summary>Triathlete</summary>

You gain an additional 20 speed each turn you can use to climb and swim.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Power Lifter</summary>

Increase carry weight by 100.

</details>
