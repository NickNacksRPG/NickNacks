# History

<details>

<summary><mark style="color:green;">Tell Story</mark></summary>

You can use your vast knowledge pool to weave together important threads in history, making for a story that is both entertaining and informative.

</details>

<details>

<summary><mark style="color:yellow;">Identify Artifact</mark></summary>

Certain items may be tied to creatures or entities from the past. By identifying the craftsmanship and symbols on items, you can determine their origin/purpose.

</details>

<details>

<summary><mark style="color:green;">Interpret Hieroglyphs</mark></summary>

Ancient people often like to communicate without using the written word. You can interpret the meaning or significance in paintings, carvings, murals, statues and the like.

</details>

<details>

<summary><mark style="color:yellow;">Recall Past Events</mark></summary>

Major battles, great tragedies and turning points in civilization are all of interest to a historian. With some kind of prompting, you can recall information related to these events dating back tens, hundreds or even thousands of years.

</details>

<details>

<summary><mark style="color:green;">Recall Prominent Figures</mark></summary>

There are some names that withstand the test of time due to their accomplishments, inventions and atrocities. With some kind of prompting, you can recall details about specific people, creatures or cultures. This can include things such as their names, species, class, nicknames, organizations etc.

</details>
