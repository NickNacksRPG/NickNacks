Fighting Styles

Snake Style

  • When a creature misses you with an attack, you can move 10 ft directly closer to it elusive. If this movement puts you within your natural reach, it Provokes. This AoO must be an unarmed strike.
  • Snake Sidewind - When you would be hit by an attack, you can spend an immediate action to parry the attack. This makes it count as a miss for snake style.

Cerberus Style

  • You cannot be flanked. When you would normally be flanked, gain a flanking bonus against the creatures threatening you.
  • Cerberus Snare - When you hit a foe with an unarmed strike, its speed is reduced by 15, it is Anchored and Grounded for 1 round On-hit.

Demon Style

  • You get Powerful Charge. You also get a +2 bonus to damage when you charge attack.
  • You can turn up to 180 degrees once during your charge.
  • Tenderize - Whenever you succeed on a drag or shove attempt, deal 10 crush damage if you move them at least 10 ft.

Grabbing Style

  • Get a +2 bonus to Grapple attacks. You only have -15 speed while grappling a creature.
  • Grabbing Everything: When you make a grapple attack, or maintain a grapple, you can make a grapple attack or maintain a grapple on another creature.

Jabbing Style

  • Whenever you make an Unarmed Attack, you can move 5 ft Elusive, so long as you still Threaten the creature you initially attacked.
  • Flurry-er - Every time you poise break a creature or land an unarmed strike against it, it gains a stack of pummel. Each stack increases your unarmed damage by 2, including poise damage. You also ignore DR against creatures with stacks of pummel.
    Pummel stacks last until the end of combat.

  • Your unarmed attacks add 2*STR to damage.

Monkey Style

  • You gain a üßóClimb speed and a "crawl" speed equal to your Land Speed.
  • You can remove the Knockdown condition as a free action.
  • Monkey Madness - When you land an unarmed strike or break a creatures Poise, you can start riding them. You follow their movement automatically until you are knocked off by poise breaking or otherwise. You have cover while riding. If an attack grazes you, it instead targets your ride at full effectiveness, as though it had hit them.
    You cannot ride creatures smaller than you.
