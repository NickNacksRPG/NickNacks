Sticky Situation Poison - A string shot spider is capable of using its spinneret as a projectile weapon. The strings are coated in powerful digestive enzymes that evaporate quickly. When they land a string shot, the creature is clumsy 1 as the web sticks to them and the surrounding terrain. At the start of each turn they are clumsy from this, they take an additional 2d6 (7) poison damage. A creature can Breakout as a swift action, removing the clumsy and caustic webs.

Weaknesses/Deep Lore

Miracle of Life - String shot spiders try to capture some of their prey alive. They attach them to the walls and cover them in webs to lay their eggs inside of their skin. Such creatures are still alive, barely, but are unable to rest or recover from the defeated condition. Within a few days, the eggs are ready to hatch and they eat their way out of their host, forming an insect swarm, but it is large and has half as much hp.

In the days leading up to the eggs hatching, a very skilled doctor can roll a DC 30 first aid check to carefully remove the eggs without killing the host. They are drained 15 and require days of bed rest before they can fully recover.
